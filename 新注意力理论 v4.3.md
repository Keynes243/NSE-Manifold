## 1. Introduction & Positioning

Sequence processing requires compressing an unbounded past into a bounded present without severing non-linear temporal dependencies. Attention-based models retain the past literally (Key-Value caches), paying memory linear in context. Linear state-space models compress the past through an LTI flow $\dot{s}=\mathbf{A}s+\mathbf{B}x$, inheriting uniform spectral decay: every memory trace erodes at rates fixed by $\operatorname{spec}(\mathbf{A})$, independent of content.

The NSE-Manifold takes a third route, **token annihilation**: a symbol acts only as a transient velocity pulse; once the pulse has committed the trajectory to a branch of a learned heteroclinic network, the symbol is discarded and the *topology of the flow itself* sustains the representation. ## 6. Variational Consistency of the Error Cascade

Fix the free energy with *frozen precisions* (the precisions $\mathbf{G}_l$ evolve on a slower timescale and are held constant under state variation — the standard quasi-static treatment of precision in predictive coding, here justified by Fenichel-type timescale separation):

$$\mathcal{F}(s) = \frac{1}{2}\sum_{l=0}^{L-1} e_l^{T}\,\mathbf{G}_l\,e_l, \qquad e_l = s_l - f(W_l s_{l+1}).$$

**Theorem 2 (Exact local gradient).** $s_l$ appears in exactly two residuals: $e_l$ (directly) and $e_{l-1}$ (through the prediction $f(W_{l-1}s_l)$). Differentiating,

$$\frac{\partial\mathcal{F}}{\partial s_l} = \mathbf{G}_l e_l - W_{l-1}^{T}\big[(\mathbf{G}_{l-1}e_{l-1})\odot f'(W_{l-1}s_l)\big] \;=\; \varepsilon_l - W_{l-1}^{T}\big[\varepsilon_{l-1}\odot f'(W_{l-1}s_l)\big] \;=\; \Psi_l.$$

Thus the force $-\kappa\Psi_l$ in Eq. (1) is exact gradient descent on $\mathcal{F}$: predictions flow **down** through $W_l$, errors flow **up** through $W_{l-1}^{T}$, and each layer's force is assembled from *adjacent quantities only* — no recursion, no global adjoint. $\blacksquare$

## 7. The Heteroclinic Skeleton: Spectrum, Attractivity, and the Stochastic Clock

### 7.1 Saddle spectrum
**Proposition 3.** For the bare competitive field $F_i(s)=s_i[1-s_i^2-\sum_{j\neq i}\Lambda_{ij}s_j^2]$, the Jacobian at the axis equilibrium $e_k$ is diagonal:

$$J(e_k) = \operatorname{diag}\big(1-\Lambda_{1k},\;\dots,\;\underbrace{-2}_{k},\;\dots,\;1-\Lambda_{d k}\big).$$

Under (A1), directions $j\in U(k)$ are expanding with rates $\lambda^{+}_{k,j}=1-\Lambda_{jk}>0$; all others contract. For $\mu_{bound}\ll1$ the barrier shifts each equilibrium and its spectrum only by $O(\mu_{bound})$ (implicit function theorem), so all conclusions persist with margins — a point v4.2 left implicit. $\square$

### 7.2 Attractivity of the channel
**Theorem 4 (Krupa–Melbourne-type criterion).** For a cycle $e_1\to e_2\to\cdots\to e_d\to e_1$ ($u=1$), define at each saddle the expansion $\lambda_k^{+}=1-\Lambda_{k+1,k}$ and the weakest transverse contraction $\lambda_k^{-}=\min_{j\notin\{k,k+1\}}(\Lambda_{jk}-1)$, with saddle value $\nu_k=\lambda_k^-/\lambda_k^+$. If

$$\rho \;=\; \prod_{k=1}^{d}\nu_k \;>\; 1,$$

the cycle is asymptotically stable (attracting heteroclinic channel).

*Sketch.* Near each saddle, the Shilnikov passage map sends an incoming transverse deviation $y$ to $y_{out}\asymp y^{\nu_k}$ (up to bounded factors); in logarithmic coordinates the return map around the cycle is affine with slope $\rho$, contracting deviations iff $\rho>1$. $\square$

**Example 7.1 ($d=3$, symmetric May–Leonard).** With $\Lambda$ cyclic, off-diagonal entries $a<1<b$: $\rho=\big(\tfrac{b-1}{1-a}\big)^3>1 \iff a+b>2$ — the classical condition, now installed as assumption (A7).

### 7.3 The stochastic clock
Deterministically, dwell times at saddles diverge as the trajectory converges into the channel — an unclocked computer. Additive noise repairs this:

**Proposition 5 (Stone–Holmes dwell law).** Under $\mathbf{d}s = F(s)\mathbf{d}t+\sigma\,\mathbf{d}\mathbf{W}_t$ with $0<\sigma\ll1$, the mean dwell time at saddle $e_k$ satisfies

$$\mathbb{E}[T_k] \;=\; \frac{1}{\lambda_k^{+}}\Big(\ln\frac{1}{\sigma}+O(1)\Big),$$

with Gumbel-type fluctuations. Token throughput is therefore $f_{clock}\approx\big[\sum_k \mathbb{E}[T_k]\big]^{-1}\propto \big(\ln\tfrac{1}{\sigma}\big)^{-1}$. $\square$

*Interpretation.* $\sigma$ is not a nuisance parameter: it is the **clock generator** of the architecture. There is a designed operating window $\sigma\in(\sigma_{min},\sigma_{max})$ — the lower edge set by acceptable latency (Prop. 5), the upper edge by routing fidelity and basin escape (§9).

## 8. Exact Dilation-Covariant Legendre Memory Transport

### 8.1 Setup
For a scalar channel $u(t)$ (applied columnwise to $s_l$), window $[t-\tau(t),\,t]$, delay fraction $r\in[0,1]$ ($r=0$ is "now"), define coefficients against the orthonormal shifted Legendre basis $\phi_k(r)=\sqrt{2k+1}\,P_k(1-2r)$, $k=0,\dots,K-1$:

$$c_k(t) = \int_0^1 u\big(t-r\,\tau(t)\big)\,\phi_k(r)\,\mathbf{d}r, \qquad \phi_k(0)=\mathbf{b}_k=\sqrt{2k+1},\quad \phi_k(1)=\boldsymbol{\pi}_k=(-1)^k\sqrt{2k+1}.$$

### 8.2 The transport theorem
**Theorem 6.** Suppose $u\in C^1$ and the in-window signal is resolved by the truncated basis (exactly, e.g. for polynomials of degree $<K$; otherwise up to the spectral truncation defect of Cor. 6.2). Then for **any** smooth window schedule $\tau(t)>0$:

$$\tau(t)\,\dot{c}(t) = \big[\mathbf{A}_0 + \dot{\tau}(t)\,\mathbf{W}\big]\,c(t) + \mathbf{b}\,u(t),$$

with constant matrices

$$\mathbf{A}_0 = \mathbf{M} - \boldsymbol{\pi}\boldsymbol{\pi}^{T}, \qquad \mathbf{M}_{kj} = \begin{cases} -2\sqrt{(2k+1)(2j+1)} & j<k,\ k-j\ \text{odd} \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$$

$$\mathbf{W}_{kj} = \begin{cases} 0 & j<k \\ k & j=k \\ (-1)^{k+j}\sqrt{(2k+1)(2j+1)} & j>k \end{cases}$$

**Proof.** Differentiate under the integral: $\frac{d}{dt}u(t-r\tau)=u'(t-r\tau)(1-r\dot{\tau})$. Substitute $u'(t-r\tau)=-\tau^{-1}\partial_r u(t-r\tau)$ and integrate by parts with $v(r)=(1-r\dot{\tau})\phi_k(r)$:

$$\dot{c}_k = -\frac{1}{\tau}\Big[(1-\dot{\tau})\boldsymbol{\pi}_k\,u(t-\tau) - \mathbf{b}_k\,u(t)\Big] + \frac{1}{\tau}\int_0^1 u(t-r\tau)\Big[(1-r\dot{\tau})\phi_k'(r) - \dot{\tau}\phi_k(r)\Big]\mathbf{d}r.$$

Insert the reconstruction $u(t-r\tau)=\sum_j c_j\phi_j(r)$ and the trailing-edge closure $u(t-\tau)=\boldsymbol{\pi}^{T}c$. Three interior matrices arise: $\mathbf{M}_{kj}=\langle\phi_k',\phi_j\rangle$ (closed form above via the classical expansion $P_k'=\sum_{j<k,\,k-j\ \mathrm{odd}}(2j+1)P_j$); $\mathbf{N}_{kj}=\langle r\phi_k',\phi_j\rangle$; and $\delta_{kj}$ from the $\dot{\tau}\phi_k$ term. For $\mathbf{N}$: integrating $\int_0^1 r\,\partial_r(\phi_k\phi_j)\,\mathbf{d}r$ by parts yields $\mathbf{N}_{kj}+\mathbf{N}_{jk}=\phi_k(1)\phi_j(1)-\delta_{kj}$, while degree counting forces $\mathbf{N}_{kj}=0$ for $j>k$; hence

$$\mathbf{N}_{kj}=\begin{cases} (-1)^{k+j}\sqrt{(2k+1)(2j+1)} & j<k\\ k & j=k\\ 0 & j>k. \end{cases}$$

Collecting terms: the $\dot{\tau}$-independent part is $\mathbf{M}-\boldsymbol{\pi}\boldsymbol{\pi}^{T}=\mathbf{A}_0$; the $\dot{\tau}$-coefficient is $\boldsymbol{\pi}\boldsymbol{\pi}^{T}-\mathbf{N}-I$, whose sub-diagonal entries cancel *exactly* against $\mathbf{N}$, leaving the upper-triangular $\mathbf{W}$ stated. $\blacksquare$

**Corollary 6.1 (Consistency).** At $\dot{\tau}=0$ the system reduces to the fixed-window translated-Legendre flow; $\mathbf{A}_0$ is related to the classical LMU/HiPPO-LegT generator by the diagonal similarity $D=\operatorname{diag}(\sqrt{2k+1})$ (and a reflection convention). Also $\operatorname{tr}\mathbf{A}_0=-K^{2}$ and $\operatorname{spec}(\mathbf{W})=\{0,1,\dots,K-1\}$: $\mathbf{W}$ is precisely the **dilation generator** on Legendre coefficients.

**Corollary 6.2 (Spectral accuracy & warp covariance).** All approximation enters through basis truncation alone; the window schedule contributes **no additional error**. For in-window regularity $H^m$, reconstruction error is $O(K^{-m})$ — superseding v4.2's fixed $O(K^{-2})$ claim (the case $m=2$). Memory is *covariant under smooth time dilations*: this is falsifiable (Prediction P2).

**Remark 6.3 (Verification by hand).** For $u(t')=t'$ and arbitrary smooth $\tau(t)$, the exact coefficients are $c_0=t-\tau/2$, $c_1=\sqrt{3}\,\tau/6$, $c_{k\ge2}=0$. Substituting into Theorem 6 with $\mathbf{A}_0=\begin{pmatrix}-1&\sqrt3\\-\sqrt3&-3\end{pmatrix}$, $\mathbf{W}=\begin{pmatrix}0&-\sqrt3\\0&1\end{pmatrix}$, $\mathbf{b}=(1,\sqrt3)^T$ reproduces $\dot{c}_0=1-\dot{\tau}/2$ and $\dot{c}_1=\sqrt3\,\dot{\tau}/6$ identically. Constants are likewise stationary. The theorem is exact on its resolved subspace.

**Remark 6.4 (Fast application).** $\mathbf{M}$ factorizes through parity-split prefix sums and $\boldsymbol{\pi}\boldsymbol{\pi}^{T}$, $\mathbf{W}$ through suffix sums, so $\big[\mathbf{A}_0+\dot{\tau}\mathbf{W}\big]C$ costs $O(Kd)$ per layer — validating the complexity table of §13.

## 9. Symbol Routing Fidelity & the Arrhenius Horizon

### 9.1 Large deviations (retained, condensed)
For $\mathbf{d}s=F(s)\mathbf{d}t+\sigma\mathbf{d}\mathbf{W}_t$, Freidlin–Wentzell theory gives escape probability from the channel's basin $\propto\exp(-\Delta E^{\ddagger}/\sigma^{2})$ per transit, with $\Delta E^{\ddagger}=\inf_\gamma \frac{1}{2}\int_0^T\|\dot{\gamma}-F(\gamma)\|_2^2\,\mathbf{d}t$ over basin-crossing paths.

### 9.2 Input-selected branching
**Proposition 7 (Routing bit-error rate).** At a saddle with $u$ expanding directions of comparable rate $\lambda^{+}$, let a token pulse of amplitude $\beta$ (with $\sigma\ll\beta\le\beta_{max}$) bias one branch during the dwell window. Linearizing each unstable coordinate as an Ornstein–Uhlenbeck race, $\mathbf{d}y_a=(\lambda^{+}y_a+\beta)\mathbf{d}t+\sigma\mathbf{d}W$ versus unbiased competitors, the probability of exiting along a *wrong* branch obeys

$$P_{err} \;\le\; (u-1)\,\exp\!\Big(-\,c_0\,\frac{\beta^{2}}{\lambda^{+}\sigma^{2}}\Big)$$

for an order-one constant $c_0$. *Sketch:* the pulse shifts the effective initial condition by $\beta/\lambda^{+}$, while accumulated noise per unstable mode has standard deviation $\sim\sigma/\sqrt{2\lambda^{+}}$; a wrong exit requires a Gaussian excursion of size $\beta\sqrt{2/\lambda^{+}}/\sigma$. $\square$

### 9.3 The corrected horizon law (Erratum E4)
**Corollary 8 (Arrhenius horizon).** Over $N$ transitions, per-step failure $p = P_{err}+P_{escape}$ yields success probability $(1-p)^N\approx e^{-Np}$. Define the **reliable horizon** $N^{*}=p^{-1}$:

$$N^{*}(\sigma,\beta) \;\gtrsim\; \min\Big\{\tfrac{1}{u-1}\exp\!\big(c_0\beta^{2}/\lambda^{+}\sigma^{2}\big),\;\exp\!\big(\Delta E^{\ddagger}/\sigma^{2}\big)\Big\}.$$

Capacity is **not** flat in $N$ (as v4.2 claimed); it decays — but with a rate exponentially suppressed in $1/\sigma^{2}$, so the horizon grows exponentially as noise (or pulse-to-noise ratio) improves. This is qualitatively distinct from LTI recurrences, whose horizon is pinned by the spectral gap of a fixed $\mathbf{A}$ regardless of noise engineering. $\square$

## 10. Invariant Measures

For $\sigma>0$, additive noise satisfies Hörmander's condition; the diffusion on the trapping ball admits a unique smooth stationary density $\rho^{\sigma}$ (the system *is* ergodic at positive noise). The structure emerges in the limit:

**Proposition 9 (Zero-noise concentration with explicit weights).** As $\sigma\to0^{+}$, $\rho^{\sigma}$ converges weakly to a convex combination of Dirac measures on the skeleton's saddles,

$$\mu^{*} = \sum_{k=1}^{d}\alpha_k\,\delta_{e_k}, \qquad \alpha_k = \frac{(\lambda_k^{+})^{-1}}{\sum_{j}(\lambda_j^{+})^{-1}},$$

*Sketch:* occupation time is dominated by saddle dwells $T_k\propto(\lambda_k^{+})^{-1}\ln(1/\sigma)$ (Prop. 5); the common $\ln(1/\sigma)$ factor cancels under normalization, leaving weights inversely proportional to expansion rates. $\square$

*Consequences.* The long-run statistics live on the zero-volume saddle graph (non-ergodicity on full $\mathbb{R}^d$ in the limit); correlation decay along the channel is controlled by $\min_k\lambda_k^{+}$, up to logarithmic dwell corrections — v4.2's purely exponential claim holds modulo these $\ln(1/\sigma)$ factors.

## 11. Capacity Theory, Restated

**Lemma 10 (Repertoire of the architecture class).** On $d$ saddles, distinct directed Hamiltonian cycles number $(d-1)!$ and length-$m$ simple paths number $d!/(d-m)!$. For each such cycle $\gamma$, the admissible parameter set $\mathcal{P}_\gamma\subset(0,\Lambda_{max}]^{d(d-1)}$ satisfying (A1)+(A7) is open and nonempty (structural stability), containing the product box with volume fraction $\Lambda_{max}^{-d}\big(1-\Lambda_{max}^{-1}\big)^{d(d-2)}$. *The $1/(e\ln d)$ coefficient of v4.2 is withdrawn (E3): it conflated the repertoire of the class with the capacity of a single parameterization.* $\square$

**Theorem 11 (Addressable itinerary capacity).** Fix one admissible $\Lambda$ with branching factor $u\ge2$ (A1). Input pulses select branches at each saddle (Prop. 7), so the set of addressable itineraries of length $m$ has cardinality $u^{m}$: the skeleton realizes an **input-addressed program space of $m\log_2 u$ bits**, at routing error bounded by Cor. 8. The factorial count of Lemma 10 measures what the architecture *class* can encode across parameterizations; $u^m$ measures what a *single trained system* can address at runtime. $\square$

**Corollary 11.1 (Memory-limited decoding horizon).** Distinguishing itineraries downstream requires the Legendre memory to resolve them: at effective precision $b$ bits per coefficient, $C_l\in\mathbb{R}^{K\times d}$ retains at most $K d\, b$ bits, so the decodable itinerary length obeys

$$m^{*} \;\le\; \frac{K\,d\,b}{\log_2 u}.$$

Skeleton capacity and memory capacity are thus *coupled resources* — a constraint absent from v4.2.
